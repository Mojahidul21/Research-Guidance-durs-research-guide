01

Mindset & Core Truths

What the speaker wants you to understand before anything else

📊
CGPA ≠ Research Ability
There is no direct relationship between your CGPA and your research capability. Professors and funding bodies care far more about what you have done than your GPA alone.
📅
Last 2 Years Matter Most
If your overall CGPA is average, your last two years' CGPA carries disproportionate weight — especially for European universities. Focus on an upward trend.
🔬
Only Research Papers Fund PhDs
For funded PhD positions, extra-curricular activities offer little advantage. Publications, thesis quality, and demonstrated research experience are what attract funding.
🌍
Geography of Opportunity
USA is highly competitive. Europe & Australia are comparatively easier entry points for funded graduate research. Europe emphasises CGPA more; USA prioritises research output.
02

Understanding Research

What research actually is — before you try to do it

Speaker's Research Area

Compound Flooding — the intersection of 4 simultaneous factors (e.g., storm surge, rainfall, river flooding, sea-level rise). This is an example of multi-variate, interdisciplinary research — the kind that attracts serious academic attention.

📄
Types of Research Output
Journal Paper — long-form, peer-reviewed, highest impact.

Conference Paper — 4–7 pages, a shorter precursor; presented at international conferences. Always given significant academic weight.

Monograph — a book-length scholarly work on a single subject.
🏆
Journal Quality Tiers
Scientific journals are ranked Q1, Q2, Q3, Q4 — Q1 being the most prestigious. Aim to publish in at least Q2 journals. Q1 publications are transformative for funding prospects.

Open Access journals are freely readable but may charge an APC (Article Publishing Charge), typically covered by your project/grant.
✍️
Author Order
First Author means you did the primary intellectual work — it carries enormous weight in academia.

Agree on authorship order before the research begins to avoid conflict later. This is a professional norm.
📈
Citations
Citation count matters for academic reputation and in some admission/funding contexts. Build a Google Scholar profile to track and showcase your citation impact.
03

The Paper Writing Process

From first draft to acceptance — what actually happens

Write thesis & paper simultaneously
A key trick: don't finish your thesis, then write a paper. Do both in parallel — they reinforce each other and save enormous time.
Abstract first — watch the YouTube video
The speaker specifically recommended finding a YouTube tutorial on abstract writing. A well-written abstract is your paper's first impression for editors and reviewers.
Submit → Desk Rejection
Some papers are rejected immediately by editors without even going to review. This is called a desk rejection — usually due to scope mismatch or poor abstract quality.
Getting Review Comments
If it passes desk review, peer reviewers send back comments. This is progress — celebrate it.
Major Revision / Rewrite
Address every reviewer comment meticulously. Use tools like Flexfix (or similar rewriting software) to assist. Non-linear, quantified writing is preferred — avoid vague language.
Acceptance
Persistence pays. If you respond thoroughly to all comments, acceptance is very achievable. "A paper will be accepted once, if you adhere."
Ethical Note

Any human subjects research requires an IRB permission certificate (Institutional Review Board / Ethical Review Board). This is standard internationally.

04

Building Research Experience

How to gain credibility before you have a PhD

🏅
Poster Competitions & Presentations
Start here. Join poster presentations and competitions at your institution or department. It is the most accessible first step into academic visibility.
🌐
International Conferences
International conference presentations carry disproportionately high weight in admissions. Track upcoming conferences — consider using an AI agent to monitor calls for papers.
📚
Read Research Papers Constantly
This is non-negotiable. Start with papers in your field. Use AI tools to accelerate comprehension. Talk to people who have published — learn how they think.
👥
Find a Team & Mentor
Search for research clubs, teams, and teachers — even at other universities. A mentor who has published is invaluable. Senior students who have published are also excellent resources.
Important Distinction

Newspaper/media writing may help in general admissions but carries little weight in securing professor-funded PhD positions. Focus your effort on academic publications instead.

05

Applying to Graduate School

The strategic and tactical side of applications

University Selection Strategy
  • Target 10 universities in total
  • 4 reach (high-ranked, competitive)
  • 3 match (medium-ranked)
  • 3 safe (lower-ranked but fundable)
Language Tests

You typically need one of: TOEFL, IELTS, or Duolingo. Check each university's accepted tests and minimum scores. Requirements vary by institution and department.

GRE — The Reality

Not all universities or departments require GRE. Even a very high GRE score doesn't guarantee funding — it merely satisfies a threshold condition. Verify per department before investing heavily in GRE prep.

Funding Seasonality (USA)
  • Fall semester: most funding available
  • Spring semester: some funding available
  • Summer: least funding — avoid applying for this entry
What Helps You Get Funded?
  • Published or submitted journal/conference papers
  • A strong, relevant thesis
  • Demonstrated research contribution aligned with the professor's work
06

Contacting Professors

The most important human step in the entire process

What to Include in Your Cold Email
  • What you have — skills, background, specific experience
  • How you can contribute to their specific research (read their papers first)
  • Your unofficial transcript
  • A 2-page resume focused on research output
  • Link to your Google Scholar profile if you have publications
🔗
Use Your Diaspora Network
Search LinkedIn or Facebook for Bangladeshi researchers at your target university. Message them to ask whether a specific professor is currently accepting students — this insider intelligence is invaluable.
📝
Write Precisely and Quantifiably
Use non-linear, quantified language. Instead of "I worked on water modeling," say "I developed a 2D hydrodynamic model of X basin covering 340 km²." Specificity signals a research mindset.
🗃️
Build a Professor Database
Systematically track professors: name, university, research area, contact status, response. A spreadsheet/database approach prevents missed opportunities and shows professionalism.
07

Essential Tools & Skills

The modern researcher's toolkit

🤖
Use AI — Properly
Use AI to read and summarise research papers, discover relevant literature, and assist in writing. Learn to use AI agents to track upcoming conferences and automate research monitoring.
🦾
OpenClaw — Must Learn
The speaker specifically emphasised learning OpenClaw (an open-source research/AI tool). Treat this as a priority skill to develop.
🎓
Google Scholar Profile
Create your profile now, even if you have no publications yet. Add your thesis, preprints, and any conference work. It is your academic identity online.
📡
Networking
Connect with teachers at other universities, join research clubs, attend talks, and engage with seniors who have published. Your network determines your access to opportunities.

08

Warnings & Pitfalls

Things that waste your time or actively hurt you

⚠ Fraud
The speaker explicitly warned about fraudulent agents, admission consultancies, and fake funding offers. Always verify independently. Real funded PhD offers come directly from universities and professors — not middlemen.
⚠ GRE Over-investment
Many applicants spend months on GRE prep for universities or departments that don't require it, or where a perfect score still doesn't guarantee funding. Check requirements per department before investing.
⚠ Extra-Curricular Over-Emphasis
Student clubs, sports, volunteering — these are good for your character but will not secure you a funded PhD abroad. Research output is the only currency that counts.
⚠ Authorship Conflict
Failing to agree on author order before beginning collaborative research is a common source of serious academic disputes. Have the conversation early and document it.
09

Long-Term Vision

Where the research path leads

Residency & Immigration

Completing a PhD abroad — particularly in the USA or Europe — opens pathways to permanent residency (PR) and Green Card eligibility. Australia also offers clear pathways. Asian countries generally do not provide comparable immigration benefits for international researchers.

The Compound Effect

Each paper, conference, and connection compounds. A thesis leads to a paper. A paper leads to a professor contact. A professor contact leads to funding. Funding leads to a PhD. A PhD leads to independence. Start anywhere. Start now.
